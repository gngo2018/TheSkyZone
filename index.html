Fun Facts of the Day

Did you know that...

  • Airplanes are designed to withstand lightning strikes. Every once per 1000 hours of flight time, an airplane is struck by lightning. However, lightning has not brought down an airplane since 1963.

  • The small hole at the bottom of windows were made to regulate cabin pressure. The windows are also made up of three acrylic panels which acts as a fail safe system if anything were to happen outside of the aircraft.

  • While in the air, the aircraft can actually fly for a certain distance without the engines being engaged. A Boeing 747 can glide up to two miles for every 1,000 feet they are above the ground.
